Web Developer

The job of a web developer might seem pretty straight forward for some audiences. They might just think you’re a developer who just creates website for clients and produce services. However it is more than that as the factors and skills you need to become a great web developer can come across anywhere in different industries. Main skills are to communicate with clients, set goals, be creative and be familiar the technology and computers. You must have good knowledge with software, applications and programming languages such as HTML/CSS, C++, JavaScript and many more. The great thing about being web developer is that you can be self-taught to become better however if you do qualify in a course or have more experience it may give you a better advantage when looking or a job. The average salary for a web developer is £25,000 but it could be more if you put the effort and time to become better than others.

Games Developer

The job of a games developer is to produce designs and create games, for computers and different platforms such mobiles and consoles. When we look at what you need to become a games developer it involves many thing such as being proficient in programming languages such as Java and C++, planning, designing, constructing the game etc. There are many different roles for a games developer as there are many parts to when you’re creating a game and all the design and concepts they come up with, all turn into code which provides operation for the game to work. Many employers specifically look for a degree in computer science for this type of job as it covers many of the fields and this shows evidence that the person is suited for the job role. The average salary for a games developer starts around £19,000 - £25,000 but it rapidly increases over the years of experience you have.



Comparison

When comparing the job role of a games developer and a web developer, there are some similarities and differences. Both of these developer roles come under the computing industry and some of the skills they do, come across both pathways. Both developers need a good understanding in programming languages as this helps both developers design and build products/services they want for clients. Moreover the skills they have is very common as both pathways need be creative, organise and have good communication when working within a team. Also both roles are time consuming as great planning and designing is needed to beat competition out in the world. The difference between these developers is that being a web-developer can be more individual based as you can produce something on your own. Whereas a games developer, you need a collective group of people working on it. Also being a game developers can take years to produce something compared to a small company website. When looking at the salary, it depends on the individual and role you’re doing on the pathway as more experience means more greater things and that is offered from both roles.
